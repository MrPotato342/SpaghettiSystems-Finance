How Tax Regime Optimization Works

In India, income tax is calculated using two parallel systems: the Old Regime and the New Regime. Both produce different tax outcomes depending on your salary structure and deduction profile.

The Old Regime typically results in lower tax when you actively claim deductions such as HRA, 80C investments, 80D health insurance, home loan interest, and other exemptions. It rewards structured financial planning.

The New Regime offers simplified slab rates with reduced complexity, but removes most exemptions and deductions. It is generally more efficient for users with low or no deductible expenses.

The optimal choice depends not only on total income, but on how your salary is structured and how much of it is eligible for deductions.

---

What Each Input Means (Important)

To correctly compare both regimes, the calculator uses the following inputs:

  • Gross Annual Income — Your total salary before any deductions.
  • HRA (House Rent Allowance) — Portion of salary eligible for rent-based tax exemption under the old regime.
  • 80C Investments — Tax-saving instruments such as EPF, PPF, ELSS, life insurance premiums (capped at ₹1.5 lakh).
  • 80D Health Insurance — Premiums paid for health insurance policies for self and family.
  • Other Deductions — Additional eligible deductions such as home loan interest, education loan interest, or professional tax.
---

The comparison works by computing your taxable income under both systems and then evaluating which regime results in lower total tax liability.

In practice, the difference is not just mathematical—it reflects a tradeoff between: simplicity (new regime) and optimization through planning (old regime).
